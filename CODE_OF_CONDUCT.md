# Community conduct

We want technical disagreement to improve the evidence, not diminish the people
providing it.

Participants are expected to be respectful, specific, and honest about what was
and was not verified. Harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language,
doxxing, threats, and sustained disruption are not acceptable in issues, pull
requests, discussions, or project spaces.

When reviewing hardware claims, challenge the artifact, measurement, provenance,
or gate result. Do not attack the contributor. Safety concerns and evidence gaps
may be raised directly and should never be suppressed to protect a launch claim.

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through GitHub. The
maintainer may edit or remove content, close discussions, or restrict
participation when needed to protect the community.
